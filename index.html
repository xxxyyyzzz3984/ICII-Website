Industries are in the process of modernizing systems and machines, ushering in a new wave of industrial revolution. The fourth industrial revolution involves the convergence of Information Technology (IT) and Operational Technology (OT) aimed at optimizing performance and production. Industrial processes are digitized by adopting emerging technologies such as robotics, Internet of Things (IoT), Machine Learning (ML), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and edge and cloud computing, etc.

Industrial Internet embodies digital technologies powering this revolution. It enables the integration of networks, computing, data analytics, sensors, industrial equipment and people. Industrial Internet creates new ways to connect sensors embedded in machines, facilities and fleets, etc. to the Internet. The large amounts of data generated from these sensors can be analyzed with predictive modeling, machine learning and data analytics to further understand the operation and condition of machines and industrial systems, and even enable autonomous operation and control of the entire systems.

Industrial Internet is applicable across a broad industrial spectrum including manufacturing, aviation, road and rail transport, power, oil and gas, healthcare, smart cities and buildings. Some of the major impacts of the Industrial Internet include the development of new and innovative services and products, which in turn leads to economic benefits. Through predictive maintenance and safety improvements, there will be greater cost reductions and time savings for industrial applications. Better connectivity also ensures that more efficient operations, risk reduction and scalability. Two major concerns with implementing the Industrial Internet is interoperability and security. Many devices with different protocols and architectures need to be able to communicate with each other. The proliferation of connected devices also means ensuring that data is transferred securely, without compromising its integrity. Latency and reliability are also critical for Industrial Internet such as smart factories.

IEEE ICII is a leading international conference focusing on Industrial Internet. The conference will cover various aspects as described above such as, but not limited to, new communication and networking technologies, new computing paradigms and architecture, data management and analytics, security, service computing and platforms, applications, and testbeds. It also aims to be a global venue for promoting interactions, dialogue and potential collaborations between academia and industry.
